Abia State, Nigeria

Abia State is located in the southeastern part of Nigeria. It was created in 1991 and is bordered by Imo, Anambra, Enugu, Ebonyi, Cross River, Akwa Ibom, and Rivers States. The state is known for its vibrant trade, rich Igbo culture, and the famous Ariaria International Market in Aba — one of the largest markets in West Africa. Umuahia serves as the state capital, while Aba is the commercial hub. Abia is also recognized for its warm, industrious people and strong community values.
